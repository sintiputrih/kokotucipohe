Tsu Surf Was Arrested For Suspected Crips Gang Racketeering

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Rapper Tsu Surf was taken into custody on RICO-related allegations on October 13 in New Jersey.

According to Fox 5 New York, Surf was captured by the US Marshals Service New York/New Jersey Fugitive Task Force around 1.30 p.m. He was booked into Newark, New Jersey’s Essex County Jail, where he is still being held.

According to accounts, Surf was arrested at his residence, where he was living with an unidentified woman. Surf attempted to flee through the backdoor, but sources said that authorities had already surrounded the house. He returned to the house after realizing this and barricaded himself inside until crisis mediators arrived and he gave himself up.

Tsu will face a judge on October 14 in federal court. Regarding the legal issues, Qua Bellamy, his manager, said to Lisa Avers that he is in good spirits and prepared to handle a problem with federal law enforcement. In a video post to her Twitter account, Avers stated:

He says he also intends to take care of his family and the people who depend on him at work. Tsu “doesn’t comprehend the enormity of the situation yet, but is prepared for the worse and praying for the best,” according to Qua.

Tsu Surf allegedly belongs to the Crips gang

Ten persons have been detained recently on suspicion of racketeering, including Tsu Surf. They are allegedly members of the Silverback Gorillas, a New Jersey-based Crips gang. Last Monday, AllHipHop.com published the first social media reports of Surf’s arrest. Contrarily, Surf tweeted on October 6th:

In recent months, other rappers have been detained on RICO-related charges, including Young Thug and Gunna. In the days to come, more people may likely face charges for comparable actions.

According to the RICO Act, anyone who is connected to an enterprise in any manner and has committed two counts of racketeering activity within ten years from a list of 35 acts may be charged with racketeering. They risk a $25,000 fine and a 20-year prison sentence if found guilty.
