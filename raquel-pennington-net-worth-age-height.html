Growing up, Pennington displayed a passion for various sports, ranging from basketball and softball to volleyball and cross country. Despite her diverse athletic interests, boxing held a special place in her heart. Graduating from Harrison High School in 2007 as the secretary of the National Honors Society, Pennington excelled academically and athletically, securing numerous scholarships. However, her dreams of collegiate sports were thwarted by a significant setback – a broken back.

Pennington’s entry into the realm of martial arts began at the age of 19, initially facing resistance from her parents who were hesitant to let her pursue a fighting career. Undeterred, she ventured into amateur MMA from 2009 to 2011, amassing an impressive record of 7 wins and 1 loss, with a notable five victories by submission. Her professional debut occurred in March 2012, marked by a TKO triumph against Kim Couture.

A significant chapter in Pennington’s career unfolded with her entry into Invicta Fighting Championships. Her promotional debut at Invicta FC 2 resulted in a unanimous decision victory against Sarah Moras, setting the stage for her continued journey in the competitive arena.

The Ultimate Fighter became a pivotal platform for Pennington in August 2013, where she showcased her skills and resilience. Her victory against Tonya Evinger, secured through a guillotine choke in the second round, marked a notable achievement. Pennington’s performance in the subsequent elimination round against Jessamyn Duke earned both participants the prestigious Fight of The Season honors.

Transitioning into the Ultimate Fighting Championship, Pennington’s promotional debut at The Ultimate Fighter 18 Finale saw her securing a unanimous decision victory against Roxanne Modafferi. Subsequent fights, such as the back-and-forth battle against Jéssica Andrade, reflected Pennington’s determination, even in the face of challenges.

A defining moment in Pennington’s UFC journey was her victory against Ashlee Evans-Smith at UFC 181, where she executed a technical submission via a bulldog choke at the end of the first round. This win marked her as the fourth fighter in UFC history to finish a fight with that move.

Pennington’s resilience and skill were further demonstrated in her encounters with renowned fighters like Holly Holm and Miesha Tate, where she emerged victorious through unanimous decisions. Despite a hiatus from fighting, Pennington returned to face Amanda Nunes in a UFC Women’s Bantamweight Championship bout at UFC 224 in 2018.

Her subsequent fights against the likes of Germaine de Randamie, Irene Aldana, and Pannie Kianzad showcased Pennington’s adaptability and determination, resulting in victories and further solidifying her presence in the competitive arena.

Beyond her accomplishments inside the octagon, Pennington’s personal life reflects her openness and authenticity. As an openly lesbian athlete, she is married to UFC Strawweight Tecia Torres.
