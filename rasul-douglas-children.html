Rasul Douglas children-American football cornerback, Rasul Douglas was born on August 29, 1995, in East Orange, New Jersey in the United States of America.

Does Rasul Douglas have any children?

Rasul Douglas and his girlfriend, Ny-Asia Franklin have a son together. The name of their son is Jeremiah Lusar Douglas and he was born on October 2, 2018. He is currently 5 years of age.

Rasul Douglas career

Most NFL draft gurus and pundits anticipated West Virginian Douglas to be selected in the third or fifth round. After completing the necessary positional drills and combine requirements, he was invited to the NFL combine.

Douglas ran the 40, 20, and 10-yard dashes again on March 31, 2017, in addition to positional exercises, when he attended West Virginia’s pro day.

Douglas, Tyler Orlosky, K. J. Dillon, Shelton Gibson, and fourteen more prospects were scouted by team representatives and scouts from all 32 NFL teams. NFLDraftScout.com rated him as the 19th-best cornerback prospect in the draft.

Douglas was chosen by the Philadelphia Eagles in the third round of the 2017 NFL Draft (99th overall). He was the second cornerback selected by the Eagles following their selection of Sidney Jones in the second round (42nd overall), and the 17th overall pick in the draft.

During training camp, he faced competition from Sidney Jones, Jaylen Watkins, Aaron Grymes, Ron Brooks, Patrick Robinson, Jalen Mills, and Ronald Darby for the starting cornerback spot.

He was listed as the Eagles’ fourth cornerback on the depth chart at the beginning of the regular season by head coach Doug Pederson, behind Ronald Darby, Jalen Mills, and Patrick Robinson.

The Carolina Panthers claimed Douglas off waivers on September 6, 2020. The team put him on the COVID-19 reserve/list on October 23 and activated him on November 2.

On April 19, 2021, Douglas signed a contract with the Las Vegas Raiders. August 23, 2021, saw his release. Douglas was signed by the Houston Texans on August 25, 2021. August 31, 2021, saw his release.

Douglas signed a contract to join the Arizona Cardinals practice squad on September 3, 2021. On October 6, 2021, the Green Bay Packers signed Douglas off the practice squad of the Cardinals due to injuries sustained by starting cornerbacks Jaire Alexander and Kevin King.

In a Week 6 game against the Chicago Bears, Douglas took Isaac Yiadom’s place as the backup cornerback when Yiadom was called for pass interference on the first drive.

On October 28, 2021, in the closing seconds of a tense game against the Arizona Cardinals, Douglas intercepted a pass intended for Kyler Murray, giving the Packers a 24–21 victory.

After suffering an injury during warmups, starting cornerback Eric Stokes was replaced by King in Week 9 against the Kansas City Chiefs.

Defensive coordinator Joe Barry started Douglas and Stokes at outside cornerback, replacing King after Stokes healed in time for the Packers’ Week 10 game against the Seattle Seahawks.

In a 36–28 victory over the Los Angeles Rams on November 28, 2021, Douglas picked up a ball from Matthew Stafford and returned it 33 yards for his first defensive score of the season.

Douglas won his first NFC Defensive Player of the Week award for his efforts. December 12, 2021: Enway to a 45–30 victory, Douglas picked off Chicago Bears quarterback Justin Fields and returned it for a touchdown, his second interception return of the game.

Douglas was in charge of two of Cleveland Browns quarterback Baker Mayfield’s four interceptions in the team’s 24–22 Christmas Day triumph. One of those interceptions came with less than a minute remaining in the game, as the Browns were trying to mount a game-winning drive.

Douglas was selected as a Pro Bowl alternate for the first time in his career due to his performance throughout the season. Douglas and the Packers agreed to a three-year, $21 million contract deal on March 19, 2022.

On October 31, 2023, the Buffalo Bills acquired Douglas in exchange for a 2024 third-round selection and a fifth-round pick in the NFL Draft.
