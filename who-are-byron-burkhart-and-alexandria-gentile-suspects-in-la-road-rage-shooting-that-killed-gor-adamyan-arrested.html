Who are Byron Burkhart and Alexandria Gentile? Suspects in LA road rage shooting that killed Gor Ada

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Young people will always get themselves entangled in situations that jeopardize their lives. This is what Byron Burkhart and Alexandria Gentile have gotten themselves into, as they have been accused of being behind a shooting incident that killed a young boy, Gor Adamyan.

Since the issue of their arrest came to light, there has been so much talk about them as people want to know who they are, and the details of the crime they have been accused of.

In this report, we will try to get as many details as we can about them, as well as the details of the crime they have been accused of and where they stand at the moment.

What Did Byron Burkhart and Alexandria Do?

In this section, we will delve into the details of what they did and what happened to them afterward.

Byron Burkhart, 29, and Alexandria Gentile, 27, have been placed under arrest for the alleged murder of 4-year-old Gor Adamyan.

They are charged with killing the victim on Friday, December 15, in Lancaster, California, by shooting him in the torso. The terrible event was labeled a road rage shooting by the authorities.

The Sheriff’s Department states that Burkhart and Gentile are currently recorded as booked into the Los Angeles County Jail.

The Adamyan family was reportedly being pursued by the suspects as they made their way to a grocery store. Mayor R. Rex Parris of Lancaster has sent the bereaved family his sympathy.

How Byron Burkhart and Alexandria Gentile Reportedly Killed Gor Adamyan

Around 7.30 p.m. local time on Friday, December 15, the tragic tragedy occurred. Gor Adamyan was traveling to get groceries when his family drove past Sierra Highway and East Avenue J in Lancaster. Gor was riding in the backseat of the vehicle.

The family’s car was cut off by another driver, according to the Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department, and they were later pursued.

After the pursuit began, the motorist approached the family vehicle and started shooting. The toddler was in the backseat of the automobile when the gunshots hit it.

Gor’s parents hurried him to the hospital right away, but he passed away from his wounds. R. Rex Parris, the mayor of Lancaster, told ABC 7 Los Angeles, “We’re praying for the family. It’s unimaginable that this could have been any of our families. It could have been any of us.”

Law enforcement officers subsequently identified Alexandria Gentile and Byron Burkhart as the culprits in the automobile. After finding their car again, the police arrested them. According to family spokesman Dr. Miguel S. Coronado, “The Christmas trees, and the presents and the food – all of that is meaningless because they don’t have their child.”

Byron Burkhart Allegedly Previously Charged With a Gun-related Felony

Dr. Miguel S. Coronado further told KTLA on Monday, “The family right now is totally devastated. They’re confused, in and out of awareness… and in disbelief that this happened to their family.”

Mayor Paris went on to discuss Byron Burkhart and the reasons he shouldn’t be allowed to walk around freely. According to KTLA, Paris said, “This particular suspect was in custody a week ago for a gun-related felony and they had to let him go because of the new bail laws.”

The new zero-bail policy, which has generated controversy since it was implemented, was another topic covered by the mayor. Many persons who are arrested for non-violent crimes are either cited and released or booked and released under this procedure.

They’re not kept under bond. Holding such individuals is crucial, according to Mayor Paris, to protect the lives of the defenseless. He added, “What did we expect? Did we really think our children weren’t going to be the victims of this [policy]? Because they are.”
