Early Life

Khabib Nurmagomedov, originally Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov, was born on September the 20th, 1988, in the village of Sidi, Tusmadinsky District, Russian SFSR, Soviet Union.

He was raised with his older brother Magomed and younger sister Amina.

The family moved from Sildi to Kirovaul, where his father converted the ground floor of their house into a gym.

He grew up in a family with siblings and cousins.

Moreover, his interest in martial arts started when watching students training at the gym.

He used to wrestle with a bear as training when he was nine years of age.

His father, Abdulmanap, was a decorated athlete and a veteran of the Soviet Army.

Not to mention Abdulmanap also wrestled from an early age before undertaking training in judo and sambo in the military.

His family moved to Makhachkala in 2001, which is the capital of Dagestan.

Khabib trained in wrestling from age 12 and judo from 15.

Career

Khabib’s career in MMA started in 2008 when he competed in Russia.

He generated 16-0 records in countries including Ukraine and Russia, and later in 2011, he moved to the Ultimate Fighting Championship.

His big debut in UFC was in 2012 against Kamal Shalorus.

After winning the debut fight, he was unstoppable. He went on defeating players like Thiago Tavares, Abel Trujillo and Gleison Tibau.

He defeated them mostly in technical knockouts and submissions.

Khabib has successfully defended his title throughout his career against fighters like Connor McGregor, Justin Gaethje, and Dustin Poirier.

In 2020 Khabib retired.

However, he never faced Tony Ferguson, who is another Lightweight title contender.

Despite his humble attitude, he has been into numerous controversies over the years.

After winning the match against the notorious McGregor in 2018, Khabib jumped over the octagon and assaulted Connor’s cornerman, Dillon Danis, leading to big chaos and fight.

After the chaos settled, the payment for the match was withheld and only released after Khabib paid the fine of $500,000. Consequently, he was suspended for nine months.
