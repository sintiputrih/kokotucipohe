Dalvin Cook | Career

Dalvin Cook did not join the football program until his second year of high school. Joseph Yearby, who is  Dalvin’s best friend, played Arizona Wildcats quarterback, and Dalvin Cook was lined up at running back.

Cook completed his prep career while leading Miami Central to a 52–5 record.

Cook also participated in “The Opening,” an all-star summer prospect camp held on the Nike Campus.

On January 1, 2014, Cook went to Florida State University. Under Jimbo Fisher, he played college football at Florida State from 2014-to 2016.

Dalvin Cook rushed 67 yards with one touchdown in his first game against The Citadel.

Cook was well regarded as the MVP of the 2014 ACC Championship Game against George Tech.

In July 2014, after being suspended for an incident outside a bar, he was found not guilty and was reinstated by Florida State in the following month.

On Oct 24, 2015, Dalvin Cook missed one game against Syracuse Orange due to his pedal ankle sprain.

He won the ACC championship in 2014 and was a second-team All-ACC selection. Dalvin Cook was first-team All-ACC and first-team All-American in 2015 and 2016.

Again on Oct 6, 2021, Cook missed a week five game against the Lions due to an ankle injury he initially suffered on week 2.

He completed his career with 4,464 rushing yards. After winning the Orange Bowl against Michigan, he would forgo his senior season at Florida State to enter the 2017 NFL Draft.

He was the third running back in the draft behind Leonard Fornette and Christian McCaffrey.
