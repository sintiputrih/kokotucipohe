Colleen Ballinger Siblings: Christopher, Trent, Rachel Ballinger

April 2024 · 6 minute read

Meet the siblings of Colleen Ballinger. The YouTuber found herself in the hot water after her former fans came forward to allege that she used the platform and the massive fame she amassed as a result, to foster inappropriate relationships with her (mostly underaged) fans.

Because of her online character, Miranda Sings, which debuted on YouTube in 2008, Colleen Ballinger gained notoriety. Miranda amassed a devoted fan base of over 10 million users on the website. She is easily recognized by her distinctive red pants and humorously overdone red lipstick. As Rolling Stone described it, Miranda’s brand of comedy is that she’s “rude, entitled, can’t sing, farts and burps for laughs, and shoots down every criticism with the unwavering belief that she’ll be famous.” Learn more about her siblings here.

Who Are Colleen Ballinger Siblings?

Colleen Ballinger is one of the four children of Tim and Gwen Ballinger, residents of Santa Barbara, California. It is the hometown of Colleen and her three siblings. Tim was born on 21 September 1959 and is 63 years old. He worked as a sales manager in Santa Barbara, California. In 1982, he graduated from California Polytechnic State University with a degree in applied science. Gwen reached age 63 on 22 August 1959. Besides Colleen, Tim and Gwen also have other three children named Christopher, Trent, and Rachel Ballinger.

Meet Christopher Ballinger, Colleen Ballinger Oldest Brother

Christopher Ballinger reached age 40 in March 2023. He was born on 16 March 1983, per Famous Birthdays.

Following his sister’s success on YouTube, Christopher started a YouTube channel of his own. His YouTube channel was launched on January 7, 2013. Since then, he has gathered over 2 million subscribers.

Chris hosts “Magic Mondays” videos on the channel in addition to his family tidbits. In addition to this, Chris has produced two DVD series under the names “The Shark” and “Monarch”. “The Jennifer Lawrence Magic Trick” was one of his most well-known videos that quickly went viral.

Christopher is married to his wife Jessica Ballinger. Known for writing and producing Haters Back Off! (2016), Bones (2005), and Davon Majors: The Show (2021), Christopher is a writer and producer.

Christopher has been married to his wife on 19 June 2004. The married couple share six children named Bailey Ballinger, Jacob Ballinger, Parker Ballinger, Duncan Ballinger, Luke Ballinger, and Teddy Ballinger.

Jessica has her own YouTube account, which she used to only post videos of her children before beginning to appear in front of the camera more frequently in late 2014. She and her family did a spoof of a Macklemore song in October 2015, and the video has amassed more than 8 million views.

Meet Trent Ballinger, Colleen Ballinger Older Brother

Colleen Ballinger’s other brother is named Trent Ballinger. He has been accused of sending inappropriate messages to a young fan of his sister’s. He has been featured on Colleen’s channels.

A user going by the name Oliver (Ollie) provided numerous screenshots of purported texts made to them by Trent when they were 13–14 years old and Trent was in his 30s in a lengthy Twitter thread that was posted on June 8th. Ollie added a screen capture to support the messages. Ollie was using she/her pronouns at the time of the messages, but now he/they pronouns are used.

Trent begins the texts by warning Ollie not to tell anyone about their talk and that anything they discuss should remain private. “Im told not to speak to people under 18,” Trent wrote. “I do so just to spread positivity.”

Trent was born on 14 February 1985. He turned 38 years old in 2023. Trent is a well-known American professional gamer who has developed several methods for achieving the end objective in video games. Because he uses hearing aids, he is known on social media as “Implanted Gamer.”

His relationship status with Rachel is unclear.

Meet Rachel Ballinger, Colleen Ballinger Only Sister

Colleen Ballinger’s only sister is named Rachel Ballinger. Born on 5 April 1991, Rachel is age 32. On September 25, 2006, she originally created her NOVAQUA YouTube account; however, it wasn’t until June 5, 2007, that she uploaded her first video.

In her “Rachel’s Room,” “Random Tuesdays,” and “Learning Sundays,” Rachel comes out as wild. She has over 832K followers on her second channel, “Rachelvlogs,” where she frequently uploads vlogs. Over 2.76 million people subscribe to Rachel Ballinger, her primary channel.

Rachel published the first “Rachel’s Room” video in 2014. She responds to questions from her admirers in this series. She also ingeniously started “Learning Sundays,” in which she teaches a subject that has been proposed by her admirers, covering everything from laundry to sex education. Anything that comes to mind that she can’t fit into her normal series is included in her “Random Tuesday/Thursday” videos.

After eight years of dating, Rachel Ballinger and her boyfriend Matt did split up in the fall of 2020. A vlogger on YouTube for years, Rachel has allowed her followers a peek into her everyday routine. Although Rachel is well known for her humorous vlogs and upbeat films about her life, it seems that she prefers to keep her love life a secret. At the end of 2020, rumors started to circulate that Rachel and her longtime boyfriend, Matt, had broken up.

When Rachel’s sister Colleen posted a TikTok video in December 2020, it led viewers to believe that the YouTuber and Matt had broken up. When it suggested in the video to “put a finger down if you broke up with someone this year,” fans saw Rachel begin to do so before she laughed and left the frame.

In one video posted in late January of 2021, Rachel told the camera: “I have not talked to my ex in over a month. We broke up in September and I have not talked to this man in over a month. I have tried because we were intertwined for eight years.” After that, Rachel began shouting, as though upset that Matt hadn’t answered all of her inquiries. She once became frustrated because she was unable to log in to remove charges on her credit card.

Fans didn’t anticipate seeing a tell-all vlog detailing Rachel’s separation from Matt because she has a history of keeping her personal life very secret. She did, however, vlog about how their breakup gave her the freedom to completely explore her sexuality in a later post. Rachel came out as gay on a vlog in February 2021.
