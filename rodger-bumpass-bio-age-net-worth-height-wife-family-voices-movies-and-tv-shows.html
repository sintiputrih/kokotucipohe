Rodger Bumpass Biography

Rodger Bumpass is an American widely known as a television, film, and voice actor who was born on November 20, 1951, in Little Rock, Arkansas, the United States as Roger Bumpass. Rodger is recognized for his long-running role as Squidward Tentacles on the American animated television series SpongeBob SquarePants, he voices several other characters on the show as well, including the Purple Doctor Fish and various anchovies.

Rodger also voiced The Chief in the animated series Where on Earth Is Carmen Sandiego?, Professor Membrane from Invader Zim, along with Mr. Besser, the school principal in the animated series The Kids from Room 402. He has many other credits in animated films, animated television series, and video games.

Rodger Bumpass Education

Rodger enrolled at Little Rock Central High School, where he received his first training in theater. Rodger attended Arkansas State University, where he majored in radio–TV and minored in theater. Rodger toiled at the campus radio station and also at Jonesboro’s Raycom Media-owned ABC-affiliated television station, KAIT-TV, where he had multiple duties as an announcer, film processor, cameraman, audio technician, as well as technical director.

While at KAIT, Rodger also wrote, produced, as well as performed in a late-night comedy program called Mid-Century Nonsense Festival Featuring Kumquat Theater. Rodger graduated from A-State in the year 1976, and when encouraged by an A-State professor to consider professional theater, he went to New York in June 1977.
