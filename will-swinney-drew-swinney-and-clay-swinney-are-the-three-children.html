Coaching Career

Alabama

During the time that he was earning his Master of Business Administration at Alabama, Swinney worked as a graduate assistant for Gene Stallings.

After earning his master’s degree in business administration from the University of Alabama in December 1995, Swinney started working as a full-time assistant coach for the Crimson Tide, where he was in charge of the wide receivers and tight ends. He continued to serve in these capacities under the leadership of Mike DuBose, who succeeded Stallings. In the beginning of 2001, he was terminated along with the rest of DuBose’s crew.

Swinney sat out the 2001 season in order to continue collecting benefits from Alabama in accordance with the terms of his contract. Rich Wingo, his former strength coach at the University of Alabama, had recently become president of AIG Baker Real Estate in Birmingham and offered him a job there. Between the months of April 2001 and February 2003, he did not serve as a coach but rather worked for AIG Baker Real Estate on a variety of real estate development projects in the state of Alabama.

Clemson

Tommy Bowden, Swinney’s previous position coach at Alabama, extended an opportunity to hire him as the wide receivers coach at Clemson in 2002. Swinney accepted the post and started working there in 2003. In addition to that, he succeeded the well-known and long-time coordinator Rick Stockstill in the role of recruitment coordinator. Swinney shown that he is an exceptional wide receivers coach as well as a recruiting coordinator by mentoring the top receivers in the ACC and being recognized as one of the top 25 recruiters in the country in 2007.
