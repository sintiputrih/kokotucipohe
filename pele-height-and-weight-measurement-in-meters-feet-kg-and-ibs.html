In Brazil, where he was born, Pelé is revered as a national hero and holds the record for most goals scored in a single season with 77. After retirement, Pelé became a political activist who worked to better the lives of the poor in Brazil as well as a global advocate for football.

There wasn’t much money in the game during Pelé’s career. As a result, the Brazilian’s football career didn’t bring in much money. Nevertheless, during his three-year tenure with the New York Cosmos in the middle of the 1970s, he was able to amass roughly $6 million.

Pelé, who retired from playing in 1977, now makes the majority of his money from sponsorship deals. With $14 million in jewelry, he earned the 13th spot on Forbes’ list of the highest-paid retired athletes in 2015.
